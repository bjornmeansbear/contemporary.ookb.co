A Mark

The Contemporary needs a mark that can do the following:

  • Be Iconic enough that people will start to recognize The Contemporary as an entity
  • Be multi-purposable so that anything The Contemporary does can be easily stamped, branded, wrapped — whatever — with the mark.
  • Stay “Contemporary” — The overriding system needs built into it the principles of evolution and flexibility

Often a brand or identity will take its basis from the location or building the institution resides in. This will obviously not be the case for the revamped Contemporary. As the model is returning to a nomadic one, the goal will instead be finding a conceptual, ethereal system that provides a vernacular to create identity materials from, but that allows any other gallery, storefront, book, kiosk, etc. to be marked in some way that is obviously “The Contemporary.”

This covers an initial plan for a greater logo system. My goal is to provide The Contemporary with the best possible solution for brand strategy. Flexibility, extendability, and a systematic approach are key to this.

A well managed graphic identity is an important tool for The Contemporary image in and around Baltimore. I think that my ideas — which look to use a reusable and reconfigurable set of forms — will do a lot to help create a unified, singular aesthetic for The Contemporary without making anything feel boring or too much the same.

Part of generating the Mark will be in creating the basis for the “system” that generates forms, etc. Some formal rules will be established … but this will not constitute final “guidelines” or “styles,” merely a starting point.

Beginnings of a style guide

From materials made so far and explorations during the Mark making stage, the basics of a style guide can be formulated and delivered to help maintain some consistency on bits and pieces that don’t require the office of KB to actually make.

    This will be multifold:
  1. How to use prescribed typefaces.
  2. How/where to use the Contemporary mark.
  3. How the “rules” for the rest of the identity system work.
  4. Constraints that must be adhered to so that all things look like The Contemporary.
  5. The initial formulation of how the contemporary mark/logotype/name might work when used with other galleries, organizations, etc
  6. Initial formulation of consistent voice of image choices, writing style, etc.
  7. Initial formulation of “signage” styles — how to mark outside of venues, insides of venues, publications, etc

This is mainly taking what we’ve made so far for the earlier steps and trying to refine it into real rules, guidelines, and recommendations. The outcome of this step will be a many page document that outlines how to use and how not to use the rules and elements created so as to maintain a consistent aesthetic front for The Contemporary.
