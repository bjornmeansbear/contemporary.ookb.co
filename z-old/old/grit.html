Overview

The Grit Fund, administered by The Contemporary as part of the Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts’ Regional Re-granting Program, supports artist-run and independent spaces, projects, publications, and initiatives in Baltimore, Maryland. The Contemporary’s mission is inspired by three guiding principles: artists matter, collaboration is key, and audience is everywhere. In keeping with this mission, the Grit Fund is intended to encourage risk-taking, emphasize collaboration, and stimulate growth – while publicly recognizing the importance and efforts of Baltimore’s alternative, self-organized arts community.

The fund supports both new and existing projects. The Grit Fund strongly prioritizes projects that present the work of multiple artists. Preference is given to projects that are unlikely to be funded through conventional sources of funding. The Grit Fund will only support proposals submitted by non-incorporated, artist-organized entities that deliberately engage a general public.

There are limited resources available to these types of unincorporated initiatives despite their crucial role to the city’s overall arts health. Recognizing these barriers,The Contemporary is awed by the perseverance and grit of these spaces and initiatives and committed to ensuring sustainability and growth, offering funding and mentorship, and working closely with this vital layer of Baltimore’s arts landscape.

Review Process and Selection Criteria

Grit Fund awardees will be announced ———. Funds will be dispersed

Proposals will be reviewed by a four person panel comprised of local and national artists and arts professionals. The Contemporary staff members do not serve on the jury.

To be considered for funding, completed applications must be submitted on time and meet all eligibility requirements. All proposals are evaluated based on criteria including:

  • Artistic Strength
    • overall vision
    • innovative, creative and conceptual strength of project
  • Feasibility
    • capacity to carry out and complete the project
    • feasibility of the project being realized within the alloted time and resources
  • Public interface
    • accessibility to the public
    • commitment
    • potential for impact on arts community and other audiences
    • providing platform / other opportunities
